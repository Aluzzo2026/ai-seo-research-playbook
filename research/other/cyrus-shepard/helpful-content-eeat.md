\# Research Insight: Cyrus Shepard - Helpful Content \& E-E-A-T



\## Overview

Cyrus Shepard (Founder of Zyppy SEO \& Former Lead at Moz) is an expert on Google’s core algorithms and quality evaluator guidelines. His research focuses on decoding the Helpful Content System and ensuring sites meet high E-E-A-T standards.



\## Key Insights \& Frameworks



\### 1. Decoding the Helpful Content System

\- Google's systems look for site-wide signals of unhelpfulness. If a site has thousands of thin, AI-generated pages created solely for search engines, the entire domain's ranking will suffer.

\- \*\*The "First-Hand Experience" Rule:\*\* Content must prove that the creator actually used the product, visited the place, or has real background knowledge in the industry.



\### 2. Actionable E-E-A-T Frameworks (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

\- \*\*Author Profiles:\*\* Build transparent, fully detailed author bio pages with links to their real social media profiles and external credentials.

\- \*\*Content Trust Signals:\*\* Include clear "Last Updated" dates, explicit editorial policies, and visible citations or links to highly authoritative reference sources.

\- \*\*User Engagement:\*\* Reduce elements that frustrate users (e.g., aggressive ads, pop-ups, slow loading times) because negative user behavioral signals correlate heavily with algorithmic drops.



\## Summary for Playbook

Cyrus Shepard warns that AI optimization must be guided by human-centric trust signals. Eradicate unhelpful content, explicitly showcase first-hand experience, and build an airtight E-E-A-T profile across the domain to bulletproof your site against core algorithm updates.
