\# Research Insight: Kevin Indig - AI Search Architecture



\## Overview

Kevin Indig (Growth Advisor \& Former VP of SEO at ClickUp) focuses on how Large Language Models (LLMs) and Generative AI are restructuring the traditional organic search ecosystem, especially for enterprise-scale websites.



\## Key Insights \& Frameworks



\### 1. The Shift from Links to Answers

\- Traditional search engines act as aggregators that direct traffic to websites via links. 

\- AI Search Architecture (like Google AI Overviews and Perplexity) acts as a "Synthesizer" that digests web content and presents the answer directly to the user.

\- \*\*Impact:\*\* Informational search intent will face a significant drop in organic Click-Through Rates (CTR).



\### 2. Large-Scale Organic Architecture Adaptation

\- For enterprise sites (e.g., ClickUp, HubSpot, G2), SEO must pivot from high-volume keyword targeting to \*\*Data Structure Optimization\*\*.

\- AI agents and search bots rely heavily on clean semantic HTML, structured data (Schema.org), and highly authoritative brand mentions to synthesize answers.

\- Programmatic SEO needs to focus on creating unique, proprietary data points that AI models cannot easily replicate or hallucinate.



\### 3. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Framework

\- Brands must optimize for "Brand Citations" within LLM responses.

\- Being cited in an AI Overview requires clear, concise formatting, direct bullet points answering specific intents, and strong digital PR to build topical authority across the web.



\## Summary for Playbook

To survive the AI search evolution, massive websites must shift their KPIs from raw organic traffic to \*\*Brand Share of Voice in AI Answers\*\* and optimize their site architecture to be easily readable by LLM crawlers.
